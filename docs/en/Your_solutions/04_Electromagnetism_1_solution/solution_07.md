# Problem 7: Cyclotron Motion of an Accelerated Electron

### 1. Stage One: Acceleration (Conservation of Energy)
The electron starts from rest and is accelerated through a potential difference $V$. The electrical potential energy lost is converted into kinetic energy.

**Given:**
* Potential Difference ($V$): $5000\text{ V}$
* Charge of electron ($q$): $1.60 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$
* Mass of electron ($m$): $9.11 \times 10^{-31}\text{ kg}$

**Equation:**
$$qV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

Solving for velocity ($v$):
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{2qV}{m}}$$
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{2(1.60 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C})(5000\text{ V})}{9.11 \times 10^{-31}\text{ kg}}}$$
$$v \approx 4.19 \times 10^7\text{ m/s}$$

---

### 2. Stage Two: Magnetic Deflection (Centripetal Force)
When the electron enters the uniform magnetic field ($B$) perpendicularly, the magnetic force ($F_B$) acts as the centripetal force ($F_c$), forcing the electron into a circular path.

**Given:**
* Magnetic Field ($B$): $0.1\text{ T}$
* Velocity ($v$): $\approx 4.19 \times 10^7\text{ m/s}$

**Equating Forces:**
$$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{r}$$

Solving for radius ($r$):
$$r = \frac{mv}{qB}$$

---

### 3. Final Calculation
Substitute the values into the radius formula:
$$r = \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31}\text{ kg})(4.19 \times 10^7\text{ m/s})}{(1.60 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C})(0.1\text{ T})}$$
$$r = \frac{3.817 \times 10^{-23}}{1.60 \times 10^{-20}}$$
**$r \approx 2.39 \times 10^{-3}\text{ m}$** (or **$2.39\text{ mm}$**)

### 4. Conclusion
The electron will follow a circular path with a radius of approximately **$2.39\text{ mm}$**. This small radius is expected due to the very high charge-to-mass ratio of the electron.
