## 7. Dynamics with Friction


#### **1. Identify the Friction Forces**
There are two surfaces where friction occurs for the 10 kg block:

**A. Friction between the 10 kg block and the floor ($f_1$):**
The normal force ($N_1$) is the weight of both blocks combined.
$$N_1 = (m_{top} + m_{bottom}) \cdot g$$
$$N_1 = (5 + 10) \cdot 9.8 = 147\text{ N}$$
$$f_1 = \mu_k \cdot N_1 = 0.2 \cdot 147 = 29.4\text{ N}$$

**B. Friction between the 10 kg block and the 5 kg block ($f_2$):**
The normal force ($N_2$) is just the weight of the top block.
$$N_2 = m_{top} \cdot g$$
$$N_2 = 5 \cdot 9.8 = 49\text{ N}$$
$$f_2 = \mu_k \cdot N_2 = 0.2 \cdot 49 = 9.8\text{ N}$$

#### **2. Calculate the Net Force ($F_{net}$)**
The applied force ($F$) is 45 N. Both friction forces oppose the motion.
$$F_{net} = F - f_1 - f_2$$
$$F_{net} = 45 - 29.4 - 9.8$$
$$F_{net} = 5.8\text{ N}$$

#### **3. Calculate Acceleration ($a$)**
Using Newton's Second Law for the 10 kg block:
$$a = \frac{F_{net}}{m_{bottom}}$$
$$a = \frac{5.8\text{ N}}{10\text{ kg}}$$
**$a = 0.58\text{ m/s}^2$**
