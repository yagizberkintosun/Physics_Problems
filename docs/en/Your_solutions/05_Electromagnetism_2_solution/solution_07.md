# Physics Problem: Wavelength and Frequency

## Problem Statement
The human eye is most sensitive to light with a wavelength of about $550 \text{ nm}$. What color does this correspond to in the visible spectrum? What is the frequency of this light?

---

## 1. Given Data
*   **Wavelength ($\lambda$):** $550 \text{ nm} = 550 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m}$
*   **Speed of light in a vacuum ($c$):** $\approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$

## 2. Physical Principles
The relationship between the speed of an electromagnetic wave ($c$), its wavelength ($\lambda$), and its frequency ($f$) is governed by the wave equation:
$$c = \lambda \cdot f$$

To find the frequency when the speed and wavelength are known, we rearrange the formula:
$$f = \frac{c}{\lambda}$$

## 3. Color Identification Analysis
The visible light spectrum for humans typically ranges from $380 \text{ nm}$ (violet) to $750 \text{ nm}$ (red). The standard approximate ranges for colors are:
*   **Violet:** $380-450 \text{ nm}$
*   **Blue:** $450-495 \text{ nm}$
*   **Green:** $495-570 \text{ nm}$
*   **Yellow:** $570-590 \text{ nm}$
*   **Orange:** $590-620 \text{ nm}$
*   **Red:** $620-750 \text{ nm}$

**Conclusion:** A wavelength of $550 \text{ nm}$ falls squarely within the **Green** portion of the spectrum. Specifically, the peak sensitivity of the human eye (photopic vision) occurs at approximately $555 \text{ nm}$, which is a yellowish-green.

## 4. Step-by-Step Calculation for Frequency

**Step 1: Convert the wavelength to SI units (meters)**
To use the speed of light in $\text{m/s}$, the wavelength must be in meters:
$$\lambda = 550 \text{ nm} = 550 \times 10^{-9} \text{ m} = 5.50 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}$$

**Step 2: Substitute values into the frequency formula**
$$f = \frac{3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}}{5.50 \times 10^{-7} \text{ m}}$$

**Step 3: Perform the division**
$$f \approx 0.545454... \times 10^{15} \text{ Hz}$$
$$f \approx 5.45 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}$$

## 5. Final Result
*   **Color:** Green
*   **Frequency:** $\mathbf{f \approx 5.45 \times 10^{14} \text{ Hz}}$
