## 1. Coulomb's Law: Point Charges in a Square

#### **1. Identify the Physical System**
* **Corner Charges ($q_{corner}$):** Four identical charges of $+1.0 \text{ C}$ each.
* **Center Charge ($q_{center}$):** One charge of $-2.0 \text{ C}$.
* **Side Length of Square ($s$):** $1.0 \text{ m}$.

#### **2. Geometric Analysis (Distance to Center)**
To use Coulomb's Law, we need the distance ($r$) from each corner to the center of the square. 
1. The diagonal ($d$) of a square with side $s$ is:
   $$d = s\sqrt{2} = 1.0 \cdot \sqrt{2} \text{ m}$$
2. The distance from a corner to the center ($r$) is exactly half of the diagonal:
   $$r = \frac{d}{2} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \text{ m}$$
3. For our force calculation, we will need $r^2$:
   $$r^2 = \left(\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\right)^2 = \frac{2}{4} = 0.5 \text{ m}^2$$

#### **3. Magnitude of Individual Forces**
The force between any one corner charge and the center charge is attractive (opposite signs). Using Coulomb's Law:
$$F = k \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}$$
$$F = (8.99 \times 10^9) \frac{|(1.0) \cdot (-2.0)|}{0.5}$$
$$F = 3.596 \times 10^{10} \text{ N}$$

#### **4. Vector Superposition & Symmetry**
Force is a **vector quantity**, meaning the total force is the sum of all individual force vectors.
* The force from the **Top-Left** corner pulls the center charge toward that corner.
* The force from the **Bottom-Right** corner pulls the center charge with the **exact same magnitude** in the **exact opposite direction**.
* These two vectors sum to zero ($\vec{F}_{TL} + \vec{F}_{BR} = 0$).

Similarly, the forces from the Top-Right and Bottom-Left corners also cancel each other out perfectly.

#### **Final Result:**
**Magnitude:** $0 \text{ N}$  
**Direction:** Undefined (The system is in static equilibrium).
