## 1. Wave Properties

#### **1. Identify the Given Variables**
The universal wave equation relates speed ($v$), frequency ($f$), and wavelength ($\lambda$):
$$v = f \cdot \lambda$$

To solve for wavelength, we rearrange the formula:
$$\lambda = \frac{v}{f}$$

* Frequency ($f$) = 440 Hz *(Note: Frequency remains constant even when the wave changes mediums)*
* Speed in air ($v_{air}$) = 343 m/s
* Speed in water ($v_{water}$) = 1482 m/s

#### **2. Calculate Wavelength in Air ($\lambda_{air}$)**
Using the speed of sound in air:
$$\lambda_{air} = \frac{v_{air}}{f}$$
$$\lambda_{air} = \frac{343}{440}$$
**$\lambda_{air} \approx 0.780 \text{ m}$**

#### **3. Calculate Wavelength in Water ($\lambda_{water}$)**
Using the speed of sound in water:
$$\lambda_{water} = \frac{v_{water}}{f}$$
$$\lambda_{water} = \frac{1482}{440}$$
**$\lambda_{water} \approx 3.368 \text{ m}$**
