# Problem: Electric Field Required for Proton Levitation

### 1. Problem Statement
We need to determine the magnitude of the electric field ($E$) required to counteract the force of gravity ($F_g$) acting on a single proton, allowing it to remain suspended (levitate) in mid-air at the Earth's surface.

### 2. Physical Principles and Given Data
For the proton to levitate, the net force acting on it must be zero ($\sum F = 0$). This means the upward **Electric Force ($F_e$)** must exactly equal the downward **Gravitational Force ($F_g$)**.

**Given Constants:**
* Mass of proton ($m_p$): $1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$
* Charge of proton ($e$): $1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* Acceleration due to gravity ($g$): $9.80 \text{ m/s}^2$

### 3. Mathematical Derivation
The gravitational force is defined as:
$$F_g = m_p \cdot g$$

The electric force exerted by a uniform electric field is:
$$F_e = q \cdot E$$

Setting the forces equal for equilibrium:
$$q \cdot E = m_p \cdot g$$

Solving for the electric field strength ($E$):
$$E = \frac{m_p \cdot g}{q}$$

---

### 4. Calculation
Substitute the given values into the derived formula:
$$E = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}) \times (9.80 \text{ m/s}^2)}{1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}}$$

First, calculate the numerator (the weight of the proton):
$$F_g \approx 1.6366 \times 10^{-26} \text{ N}$$

Now, divide by the charge:
$$E = \frac{1.6366 \times 10^{-26}}{1.60 \times 10^{-19}}$$
$$E \approx 1.022 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}$$

### 5. Conclusion
The required electric field strength is approximately **$1.02 \times 10^{-7} \text{ N/C}$**. 

**Directional Note:** Since a proton is positively charged, the electric force follows the direction of the field lines. Therefore, the electric field must be directed **upward** to oppose gravity.
