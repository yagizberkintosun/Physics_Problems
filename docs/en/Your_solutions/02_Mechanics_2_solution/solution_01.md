## 1. Gravitational Dependence of a Simple Pendulum



#### **Part 1: Period on the Moon**
The period $T$ of a simple pendulum is given by the formula:
$$T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

Since the length $L$ of the pendulum remains constant, the relationship between the period and gravity is:
$$T \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{g}}$$

We can set up a ratio between the Moon and Earth:
$$\frac{T_{Moon}}{T_{Earth}} = \sqrt{\frac{g_{Earth}}{g_{Moon}}}$$

Given that $g_{Moon} = \frac{1}{6} g_{Earth}$, the ratio becomes:
$$\frac{T_{Moon}}{4} = \sqrt{\frac{g_{Earth}}{\frac{1}{6}g_{Earth}}} = \sqrt{6}$$

Solving for $T_{Moon}$:
$$T_{Moon} = 4 \cdot \sqrt{6} \approx 4 \cdot 2.449$$
$$T_{Moon} \approx 9.80\text{ s}$$

#### **Part 2: Required Length on Earth**
Using the period formula, we solve for length $L$:
$$L = \frac{T^2 \cdot g}{4\pi^2}$$

Substituting the known values ($T = 1\text{ s}$, $g \approx 9.81\text{ m/s}^2$):
$$L = \frac{(1)^2 \cdot 9.81}{4\pi^2}$$
$$L \approx \frac{9.81}{39.478}$$
$$L \approx 0.248\text{ m} \text{ (or } 24.8\text{ cm)}$$
