## 4. Energy & Momentum

#### **1. Find the speed of the first block before the collision**
First, we use Conservation of Energy to find how fast the first block is moving right before it hits the second one.
* Mass 1 ($m_1$) = 0.5 kg
* Height ($h$) = 3.0 m
* Gravity ($g$) $\approx$ 9.81 m/s²

$$PE_{top} = KE_{bottom}$$
$$m_1gh = \frac{1}{2}m_1v_1^2$$

Just like our pendulum problem, the mass cancels out:
$$v_1 = \sqrt{2gh}$$
$$v_1 = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.81 \cdot 3.0}$$
$$v_1 = \sqrt{58.86}$$
**$v_1 \approx 7.67 \text{ m/s}$**

#### **2. Apply Conservation of Momentum for the collision**
When the $0.5\text{ kg}$ block hits the $1.5\text{ kg}$ block and they stick together, this is called a **perfectly inelastic collision**. Momentum is conserved.
* Mass 2 ($m_2$) = 1.5 kg
* Initial velocity of $m_2$ = 0 m/s (it's at rest)

The formula for conservation of momentum is:
$$p_{initial} = p_{final}$$
$$m_1v_1 + m_2(0) = (m_1 + m_2)v_f$$

Substitute the values we know:
$$0.5 \cdot 7.67 = (0.5 + 1.5) \cdot v_f$$
$$3.835 = 2.0 \cdot v_f$$

#### **3. Solve for Final Velocity ($v_f$)**
Now we just divide by the total combined mass to find their final speed together:
$$v_f = \frac{3.835}{2.0}$$
**$v_f \approx 1.92 \text{ m/s}$**
