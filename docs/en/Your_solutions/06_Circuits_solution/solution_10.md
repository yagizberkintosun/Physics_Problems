# Physics Problem: Average Current of a Lightning Bolt

## 1. Problem Statement
A lightning bolt transfers a total charge of **30 Coulombs** to the ground in a time interval of **2 milliseconds**. We need to calculate the **average current** of this lightning bolt.

---

## 2. Fundamental Physics Concept

### Average Current
Electric current ($I$) is defined as the rate at which electric charge flows through a cross-sectional area. The **average current** is the total charge transferred divided by the total time taken for the transfer:

$$I_{avg} = \frac{\Delta Q}{\Delta t}$$

Where:
*   $I$ is the current measured in Amperes (A).
*   $Q$ is the quantity of charge in Coulombs (C).
*   $t$ is the time in seconds (s).

---

## 3. Given Variables & Unit Conversion
To obtain the result in standard units (Amperes), we must convert the time from milliseconds to seconds.

*   **Total Charge ($\Delta Q$):** $30\ \text{C}$
*   **Time Interval ($\Delta t$):** $2\ \text{ms}$

**Conversion:**
Since $1\ \text{ms} = 0.001\ \text{s}$ (or $10^{-3}\ \text{s}$), we calculate:
$$\Delta t = 2 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{s} = 0.002\ \text{s}$$

---

## 4. Step-by-Step Calculation

### Step A: Apply the Formula
Substitute the known values into the equation for average current:
$$I_{avg} = \frac{30\ \text{C}}{0.002\ \text{s}}$$

### Step B: Simplify the Arithmetic
To make the division easier, you can think of $0.002$ as $\frac{2}{1000}$:
$$I_{avg} = 30 \div \frac{2}{1000}$$
$$I_{avg} = 30 \times \frac{1000}{2}$$
$$I_{avg} = 15,000\ \text{A}$$

---

## 5. Final Result
The average current of the lightning bolt described in **image_1ca6d6.png** is:
**$$I_{avg} = 15,000\ \text{A} \text{ (or 15 kA)}$$**

**Teacher's Note:** While 30 Coulombs is a relatively small amount of total charge, the extremely short discharge time ($0.002\ \text{s}$) results in a massive current. This high amperage is what causes the intense heat, light, and mechanical damage associated with lightning strikes.
