## 2. Systems of Equations

Solve for $x$ and $y$:
1) $2x + 3y = 12$
2) $x - y = 1$

---

### Step 1: Isolate one variable
From equation (2), we can easily express $x$ in terms of $y$:
$$x = y + 1$$

### Step 2: Substitute into the other equation
Substitute $(y + 1)$ for $x$ in equation (1):
$$2(y + 1) + 3y = 12$$

### Step 3: Solve for $y$
Expand and simplify:
$$2y + 2 + 3y = 12$$
$$5y + 2 = 12$$
$$5y = 10$$
$$\mathbf{y = 2}$$

### Step 4: Solve for $x$
Plug $y = 2$ back into our isolated equation for $x$:
$$x = 2 + 1$$
$$\mathbf{x = 3}$$

---

### Graphical Representation
The solution $(3, 2)$ represents the point where these two lines intersect on a Cartesian plane.



**Final Solution:** $(3, 2)$
