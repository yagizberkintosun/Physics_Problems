## 7. Standing Wave Modes

#### **1. Identify the Given Variables**
* Length of the string ($L$) = 80 cm = 0.8 m
* Number of antinodes ($n$) = 4

#### **2. Relate Length to Wavelength**
For a standing wave on a string, each "loop" or antinode corresponds to exactly half of a wavelength ($\frac{\lambda}{2}$). 
Because there are 4 antinodes, the total length of the string is equal to 4 half-wavelengths:
$$L = n \cdot \frac{\lambda}{2}$$
$$L = 4 \cdot \frac{\lambda}{2}$$
$$L = 2\lambda$$

#### **3. Solve for Wavelength ($\lambda$)**
Now we just rearrange the simplified formula to solve for $\lambda$ and plug in our length:
$$\lambda = \frac{L}{2}$$
$$\lambda = \frac{0.8}{2}$$

**Result:**
**$\lambda = 0.4 \text{ m}$** *(or 40 cm)*
