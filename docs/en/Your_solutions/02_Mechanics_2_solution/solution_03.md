## 3. Conservation of Energy


#### **1. Identify the Height ($h$)**
To find the speed, we first need the vertical distance the bob drops. 
* Length $L = 1.0\text{ m}$
* Angle $\theta = 15^\circ$

The vertical height $h$ from the bottom of the swing is:
$$h = L - L \cos(\theta)$$
$$h = 1.0 - 1.0 \cos(15^\circ)$$
$$h = 1.0 - 0.9659 = 0.0341\text{ m}$$

#### **2. Apply Conservation of Energy**
At the release point, the energy is all Potential ($PE$). At the bottom, it is all Kinetic ($KE$).
$$PE_{top} = KE_{bottom}$$
$$mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

#### **3. Solve for Velocity ($v$)**
The mass $m$ cancels out:
$$gh = \frac{1}{2}v^2$$
$$v = \sqrt{2gh}$$

Substituting the values ($g \approx 9.81\text{ m/s}^2$):
$$v = \sqrt{2 \cdot 9.81 \cdot 0.0341}$$
$$v = \sqrt{0.669}$$
**$v \approx 0.818\text{ m/s}$**
