## 5. Echo Ranging (Detailed Solution)

#### **1. State Assumptions and Given Variables**
Before calculating, we must establish the physical conditions of the system:
* We assume the sound wave travels through a homogeneous medium (air) at a constant velocity.
* We assume the effect of wind or atmospheric shifts is negligible.
* **Speed of sound in air ($v$):** 343 m/s
* **Total time for the echo ($t_{total}$):** 1.0 s

#### **2. Define the Physics of the Echo (The Round Trip)**
An echo represents a complete "round trip" for a sound wave. The sound must travel from the person's vocal cords to the cliff, reflect off the rock surface, and travel the exact same path back to the person's ear. 
* Let $d$ represent the one-way distance from the person to the cliff. 
* Therefore, the total distance the sound wave travels is twice that distance ($2d$).

#### **3. Formulate the Equation**
Since the speed of sound is constant in this scenario, we use the fundamental kinematic equation for zero acceleration:
$$\text{Total Distance} = \text{Velocity} \cdot \text{Total Time}$$
    
Substituting our specific physics variables into the equation gives us:
$$2d = v \cdot t_{total}$$

#### **4. Isolate the Target Variable ($d$)**
Following standard physics problem-solving protocols, we algebraically isolate the variable we are looking for *before* substituting any numerical values. Divide both sides by 2:
$$d = \frac{v \cdot t_{total}}{2}$$

#### **5. Substitute Values and Solve**
Now, substitute the given values into our rearranged equation to find the final distance:
$$d = \frac{343 \cdot 1.0}{2}$$
$$d = \frac{343}{2}$$
    
**Result:**
**$d = 171.5 \text{ m}$**

*(Conclusion: The sound wave travels a total of 343 meters, meaning the cliff is exactly 171.5 meters away from the source).*
