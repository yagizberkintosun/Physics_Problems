## 5. Inelastic Collision



### **Step-by-Step Solution**

#### **1. Calculate Final Speed (Conservation of Momentum)**
In an isolated system, the total momentum before the collision equals the total momentum after.
* Mass of runner (m1): 70 kg
* Velocity of runner (v1): 3 m/s
* Mass of cart (m2): 140 kg
* Velocity of cart (v2): 0 m/s (stationary)

**Equation:**
m1*v1 + m2*v2 = (m1 + m2) * vf

**Calculation:**
(70 kg * 3 m/s) + (140 kg * 0 m/s) = (70 kg + 140 kg) * vf
210 kg*m/s = 210 kg * vf
vf = 1 m/s

**The final speed of the cart and runner is 1 m/s.**

#### **2. Determine if Kinetic Energy (KE) is Conserved**
We calculate the total KE before and after the collision.

**KE Initial:**
KE_i = 0.5 * m1 * v1^2
KE_i = 0.5 * 70 * (3)^2
KE_i = 0.5 * 70 * 9 = 315 J

**KE Final:**
KE_f = 0.5 * (m1 + m2) * vf^2
KE_f = 0.5 * (210) * (1)^2
KE_f = 0.5 * 210 * 1 = 105 J

#### **Conclusion:**
Kinetic energy is **not conserved**. 
The initial energy was 315 J, but the final energy is only 105 J. The "lost" energy (210 J) was converted into non-mechanical forms such as heat, sound, and the internal work required to couple the runner to the cart.
