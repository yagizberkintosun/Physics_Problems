## 3. Proportionality: Universal Law of Gravitation

The gravitational force is given by the formula:
$$F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$



---

### Given Changes
* **Masses:** Both $m_1$ and $m_2$ are halved:
    * $m_1' = \frac{1}{2}m_1$
    * $m_2' = \frac{1}{2}m_2$
* **Distance:** The distance $r$ is doubled:
    * $r' = 2r$

### Step 1: Substitute the new values into the equation
Let the new force be $F'$:
$$F' = G \frac{(\frac{1}{2}m_1) \cdot (\frac{1}{2}m_2)}{(2r)^2}$$

### Step 2: Simplify the numerator and denominator
* **Numerator:** $(\frac{1}{2}) \cdot (\frac{1}{2}) = \frac{1}{4}$
* **Denominator:** $(2r)^2 = 4r^2$

$$F' = G \frac{\frac{1}{4} m_1 m_2}{4r^2}$$

### Step 3: Calculate the overall factor
Factor out the constants:
$$F' = \left( \frac{1/4}{4} \right) \cdot \left( G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \right)$$
$$F' = \frac{1}{16} \cdot F$$

---

### Conclusion
If the distance is doubled and both masses are halved, the gravitational force $F$ changes by a factor of **$1/16$** (it becomes 16 times weaker).
