# Physics Problem: Power and Energy Dissipation

## 1. Problem Statement
We have the following parameters:
*   **Resistance ($R$):** **100 Ω**
*   **Voltage ($V$):** **50 V**
*   **Time ($t$):** **5 minutes**

**Goal:** Calculate the total power dissipated by the resistor and the total energy consumed during the specified time interval.

---

## 2. Fundamental Physics Principles

### Joule's Law (Power)
Power ($P$) is the rate at which electrical energy is converted into heat within a resistor. Given the voltage and resistance, we use the formula:
$$P = \frac{V^2}{R}$$

### Electrical Energy
The total energy ($E$) consumed is the product of the power and the time for which it is applied:
$$E = P \cdot t$$
*Note: To calculate energy in Joules (**J**), time must be converted into the SI unit of seconds (**s**).*

---

## 3. Step-by-Step Calculation

### Step A: Calculate Power Dissipated
Substitute the values from **image_1c549a.png** into the power equation:
$$P = \frac{(50\ \text{V})^2}{100\ \Omega}$$
$$P = \frac{2500}{100}$$
**$P = 25\ \text{W}$**

### Step B: Convert Time to Seconds
Since the problem provides time in minutes, we convert it to seconds:
$$t = 5\ \text{minutes} \times 60\ \text{seconds/minute} = 300\ \text{s}$$

### Step C: Calculate Total Energy Consumed
Multiply the power by the total time:
$$E = 25\ \text{W} \times 300\ \text{s}$$
**$E = 7500\ \text{J}$**

---

## 4. Final Results Summary

| Parameter | Value |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Power Dissipated ($P$)** | **25 W** |
| **Energy Consumed ($E$)** | **7500 J** (or **7.5 kJ**) |

**Teacher's Note:** The power dissipation of **25 W** means the resistor is converting **25 Joules** of electrical energy into thermal energy every second. Over the full **300-second** duration, this results in a total energy consumption of **7500 J**.
