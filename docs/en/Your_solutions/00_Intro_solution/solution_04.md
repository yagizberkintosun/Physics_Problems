# Physics Problem: Rearranging Formulas

## 4. Rearranging Formulas: Simple Pendulum

The period $T$ of a simple pendulum is given by:
$$T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$



---

### Step-by-Step Rearrangement to Isolate $g$:

1. **Isolate the square root term:**
   Divide both sides by $2\pi$:
   $$\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

2. **Remove the square root:**
   Square both sides of the equation:
   $$\left(\frac{T}{2\pi}\right)^2 = \frac{L}{g}$$
   $$\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = \frac{L}{g}$$

3. **Invert the equation (or cross-multiply):**
   To move $g$ to the numerator, we can flip both sides:
   $$\frac{4\pi^2}{T^2} = \frac{g}{L}$$

4. **Solve for $g$:**
   Multiply both sides by $L$:
   $$g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}$$

---

### Final Formula
The acceleration due to gravity $g$ is expressed as:
$$\mathbf{g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}}$$
