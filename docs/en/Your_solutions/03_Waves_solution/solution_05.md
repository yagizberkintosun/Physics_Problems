## 5. Echo Ranging

#### **1. Identify the Given Variables**
* Total time for the echo ($t_{total}$) = 1 s
* Speed of sound in air ($v$) = 343 m/s

#### **2. Account for the Round Trip**
An echo is a sound wave that travels to a barrier (the cliff) and then bounces back to the source. That means the $1\text{ second}$ total time includes both the trip *there* and the trip *back*. 

To find the time it takes to just reach the cliff, we cut the total time in half:
$$t_{one-way} = \frac{t_{total}}{2}$$
$$t_{one-way} = \frac{1}{2} = 0.5 \text{ s}$$

#### **3. Calculate the Distance ($d$)**
Now we just use the basic kinematic formula for constant velocity (Distance = Speed $\cdot$ Time) using our one-way time:
$$d = v \cdot t_{one-way}$$
$$d = 343 \cdot 0.5$$

**Result:**
**$d = 171.5 \text{ m}$**
