## 10. Force Field and Power



#### **1. Velocity ($\mathbf{v}$)**
Velocity is the first derivative of position with respect to time ($v = \frac{dr}{dt}$).
* $v_x = \frac{d}{dt}(5t^2 - t) = 10t - 1$
* $v_y = \frac{d}{dt}(2t^3) = 6t^2$
* $v_z = \frac{d}{dt}(-3t + 2) = -3$

**Vector Form:**
$$\mathbf{v}(t) = (10t - 1)\mathbf{i} + (6t^2)\mathbf{j} - 3\mathbf{k}$$

#### **2. Momentum ($\mathbf{p}$)**
Momentum is mass times velocity ($\mathbf{p} = m\mathbf{v}$). The mass is 0.5 kg.
* $p_x = 0.5 \cdot (10t - 1) = 5t - 0.5$
* $p_y = 0.5 \cdot (6t^2) = 3t^2$
* $p_z = 0.5 \cdot (-3) = -1.5$

**Vector Form:**
$$\mathbf{p}(t) = (5t - 0.5)\mathbf{i} + 3t^2\mathbf{j} - 1.5\mathbf{k}$$

#### **3. Acceleration ($\mathbf{a}$)**
Acceleration is the derivative of velocity with respect to time ($\mathbf{a} = \frac{d\mathbf{v}}{dt}$).
* $a_x = \frac{d}{dt}(10t - 1) = 10$
* $a_y = \frac{d}{dt}(6t^2) = 12t$
* $a_z = \frac{d}{dt}(-3) = 0$

**Vector Form:**
$$\mathbf{a}(t) = 10\mathbf{i} + 12t\mathbf{j}$$
