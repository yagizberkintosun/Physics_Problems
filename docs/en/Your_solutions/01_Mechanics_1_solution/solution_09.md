# Momentum Comparison Analysis

This document calculates and compares the linear momentum of two different objects: a fly and a tennis ball.

---

## 1. The Momentum Formula
Linear momentum ($p$) is defined as the product of an object's mass ($m$) and its velocity ($v$):

$$p = m \cdot v$$

**Standard Units:**
* Mass ($m$): Kilograms (kg)
* Velocity ($v$): Meters per second (m/s)
* Momentum ($p$): Kilogram-meters per second (kg·m/s)

---

## 2. Calculations

### Object A: The Fly
* **Mass:** $2 \text{ g} = 0.002 \text{ kg}$
* **Velocity:** $10 \text{ m/s}$

$$p_{fly} = 0.002 \text{ kg} \times 10 \text{ m/s}$$
$$p_{fly} = 0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$$

### Object B: The Tennis Ball
* **Mass:** $60 \text{ g} = 0.06 \text{ kg}$
* **Velocity:** $1 \text{ m/s}$

$$p_{ball} = 0.06 \text{ kg} \times 1 \text{ m/s}$$
$$p_{ball} = 0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$$

---

## 3. Comparison and Conclusion
Comparing the two results:
$$0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s} > 0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$$

The **tennis ball** has greater momentum. Even though the fly is moving ten times faster, the tennis ball's mass is thirty times greater, resulting in a higher total momentum.
