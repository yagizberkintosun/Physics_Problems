## 6. Wave Equation

#### **1. Identify the Standard Wave Equation**
The standard mathematical form of a traveling wave is:
$$y(x,t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t)$$

By comparing this to our given equation $y(x,t) = 0.05 \sin(2\pi x - 50\pi t)$, we can extract the core variables directly:
* **Amplitude ($A$)** = 0.05
* **Wave number ($k$)** = $2\pi$
* **Angular frequency ($\omega$)** = $50\pi$

#### **2. Solve for Each Property**

**a) Amplitude ($A$)**
The amplitude is the maximum displacement, which we just read directly from the front of the equation.
**$A = 0.05 \text{ m}$**

**b) Wavelength ($\lambda$)**
The wave number ($k$) is related to wavelength by the formula $k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$.
$$\lambda = \frac{2\pi}{k}$$
$$\lambda = \frac{2\pi}{2\pi}$$
**$\lambda = 1 \text{ m}$**

**c) Frequency ($f$)**
The angular frequency ($\omega$) is related to regular frequency by the formula $\omega = 2\pi f$.
$$f = \frac{\omega}{2\pi}$$
$$f = \frac{50\pi}{2\pi}$$
**$f = 25 \text{ Hz}$**

**d) Wave speed ($v$)**
The speed of the wave can be found using the formula $v = f \cdot \lambda$, or by simply dividing $\omega$ by $k$.
$$v = \frac{\omega}{k}$$
$$v = \frac{50\pi}{2\pi}$$
**$v = 25 \text{ m/s}$**
