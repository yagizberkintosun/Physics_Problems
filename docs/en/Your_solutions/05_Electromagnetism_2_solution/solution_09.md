# Physics Problem: Refraction (Snell's Law)

## Problem Statement
A light ray travels from air ($n = 1.00$) into glass ($n = 1.50$). If the angle of incidence is $30^\circ$, what is the angle of refraction?

---

## 1. Given Data
*   **Index of refraction of air ($n_1$):** $1.00$
*   **Index of refraction of glass ($n_2$):** $1.50$
*   **Angle of incidence ($\theta_1$):** $30^\circ$

## 2. Physical Principles
The behavior of light as it passes from one medium to another is described by **Snell's Law**:
$$n_1 \sin(\theta_1) = n_2 \sin(\theta_2)$$

Where:
*   $n_1$ and $n_2$ are the refractive indices of the two media.
*   $\theta_1$ is the angle of incidence (measured from the normal).
*   $\theta_2$ is the angle of refraction (measured from the normal).

## 3. Conceptual Analysis
Because the light is moving from a lower refractive index (air) to a higher refractive index (glass), the light ray will **slow down** and **bend toward the normal**. Consequently, we expect the angle of refraction ($\theta_2$) to be smaller than the angle of incidence ($30^\circ$).

## 4. Step-by-Step Calculation

**Step 1: Rearrange Snell's Law to solve for $\sin(\theta_2)$**
$$\sin(\theta_2) = \frac{n_1 \sin(\theta_1)}{n_2}$$

**Step 2: Substitute the known values**
Given $\sin(30^\circ) = 0.5$:
$$\sin(\theta_2) = \frac{1.00 \cdot \sin(30^\circ)}{1.50}$$
$$\sin(\theta_2) = \frac{0.5}{1.50}$$

**Step 3: Simplify the fraction**
$$\sin(\theta_2) = \frac{1}{3} \approx 0.3333$$

**Step 4: Solve for $\theta_2$ using the inverse sine function**
$$\theta_2 = \arcsin(0.3333)$$
$$\theta_2 \approx 19.4712^\circ$$

## 5. Final Result
Rounding to two decimal places, the angle of refraction is:

$$\mathbf{\theta_2 \approx 19.47^\circ}$$
