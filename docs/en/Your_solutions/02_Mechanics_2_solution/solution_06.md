## 6. Energy Dissipation


#### **1. Understand the Relationship between Energy and Height**
The mechanical energy ($E$) at the peak of a bounce is entirely gravitational potential energy:
$$E = mgh$$
Since mass ($m$) and gravity ($g$) are constant, the height ($h$) is directly proportional to the energy ($E$):
$$h \propto E$$

#### **2. Determine the Retention Factor**
If the ball loses $30\%$ of its energy, it retains $70\%$:
$$\text{Retention Factor} = 1.00 - 0.30 = 0.70$$

#### **3. Calculate Height after Each Bounce**
* **Initial Height ($h_0$):** $2.0\text{ m}$
* **After 1st Bounce ($h_1$):**
  $$h_1 = h_0 \cdot 0.70$$
  $$h_1 = 2.0 \cdot 0.70 = 1.4\text{ m}$$
* **After 2nd Bounce ($h_2$):**
  $$h_2 = h_1 \cdot 0.70$$
  $$h_2 = 1.4 \cdot 0.70 = 0.98\text{ m}$$

**Alternative Method (Power Rule):**
$$h_n = h_0 \cdot (0.70)^n$$
$$h_2 = 2.0 \cdot (0.70)^2$$
$$h_2 = 2.0 \cdot 0.49 = 0.98\text{ m}$$

**The ball rises to a height of 0.98 meters after the second bounce.**
