# Problem: Comparison of Electric and Gravitational Forces in a Hydrogen Atom

### 1. Given Data and Constants
To compare these forces, we use the following standard physical constants:
* **Distance ($r$):** $5.3 \times 10^{-11} \text{ m}$ (Bohr radius)
* **Elementary Charge ($e$):** $1.60 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* **Mass of Proton ($m_p$):** $1.67 \times 10^{-27} \text{ kg}$
* **Mass of Electron ($m_e$):** $9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$
* **Coulomb's Constant ($k$):** $8.99 \times 10^9 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$
* **Gravitational Constant ($G$):** $6.67 \times 10^{-11} \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{kg}^2$

---

### 2. Magnitude of Electric Force ($F_e$)
According to **Coulomb's Law**, the force of attraction between the proton and electron is:
$$F_e = k \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}$$

Substituting the values:
$$F_e = \frac{(8.99 \times 10^9) \times (1.60 \times 10^{-19})^2}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}$$
$$F_e = \frac{2.301 \times 10^{-28}}{2.809 \times 10^{-21}}$$
**$F_e \approx 8.19 \times 10^{-8} \text{ N}$**

---

### 3. Magnitude of Gravitational Force ($F_g$)
According to **Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation**:
$$F_g = G \frac{m_e m_p}{r^2}$$

Substituting the values:
$$F_g = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (9.11 \times 10^{-31}) \times (1.67 \times 10^{-27})}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}$$
$$F_g = \frac{1.015 \times 10^{-67}}{2.809 \times 10^{-21}}$$
**$F_g \approx 3.61 \times 10^{-47} \text{ N}$**

---

### 4. Ratio Comparison ($F_e / F_g$)
To find the relative strength, we divide the electric force by the gravitational force:
$$\text{Ratio} = \frac{F_e}{F_g} = \frac{8.19 \times 10^{-8}}{3.61 \times 10^{-47}}$$
$$\text{Ratio} \approx 2.27 \times 10^{39}$$

### 5. Conclusion
The electric force is approximately **$2.27 \times 10^{39}$ times stronger** than the gravitational force. This confirms that at the atomic scale, gravity is entirely negligible.
