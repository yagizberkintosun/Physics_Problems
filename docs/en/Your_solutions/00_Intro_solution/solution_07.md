## 7. Logic & Series: The Fly and the Bicycle

**Problem Statement:**
* Bicycle distance to wall: $d = 10\text{ m}$
* Bicycle speed: $v_b = 1\text{ m/s}$
* Fly speed: $v_f = 2\text{ m/s}$



---

### The Logical Approach (The "Time" Method)
Instead of calculating every individual trip the fly makes (which forms an infinite geometric series), we can simply determine how long the fly is in the air.

#### Step 1: Calculate the time until the bicycle hits the wall
The bicycle moves at a constant speed toward the wall. The time ($t$) it takes to cover the distance is:
$$t = \frac{\text{distance}}{\text{speed}} = \frac{10\text{ m}}{1\text{ m/s}}$$
$$t = 10\text{ seconds}$$

#### Step 2: Calculate the total distance the fly travels
The fly is flying at a constant speed of $2\text{ m/s}$ for the entire duration that the bicycle is moving. Since the fly doesn't stop until the bicycle hits the wall:
$$\text{Distance}_{\text{fly}} = \text{speed}_{\text{fly}} \times t$$
$$\text{Distance}_{\text{fly}} = 2\text{ m/s} \times 10\text{ s}$$
$$\mathbf{\text{Distance}_{\text{fly}} = 20\text{ meters}}$$

---

### Conclusion
The fly travels a total distance of **20 meters** before being crushed against the wall.
