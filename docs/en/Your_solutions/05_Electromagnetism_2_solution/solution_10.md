# Physics Problem: Speed of Light in Media

## Problem Statement
What is the speed of light in a diamond, which has an index of refraction $n = 2.42$?

---

## 1. Given Data
*   **Index of refraction of diamond ($n$):** $2.42$
*   **Speed of light in a vacuum ($c$):** $\approx 3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$

## 2. Physical Principles
The **index of refraction** ($n$) of a material is a dimensionless number that describes how fast light travels through that material relative to its speed in a vacuum. It is defined by the formula:
$$n = \frac{c}{v}$$

Where:
*   $c$ is the speed of light in a vacuum.
*   $v$ is the phase velocity of light in the medium.

To find the speed in the medium ($v$), we rearrange the formula:
$$v = \frac{c}{n}$$

## 3. Conceptual Note
A higher index of refraction indicates a more "optically dense" medium, meaning light will travel significantly slower in that medium than in a vacuum. Diamond has one of the highest refractive indices of any transparent substance, which contributes to its brilliance.

## 4. Step-by-Step Calculation

**Step 1: Substitute the known values into the rearranged formula**
$$v = \frac{3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}}{2.42}$$

**Step 2: Perform the division**
$$v \approx 1.239669 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$$

**Step 3: Apply significant figures**
Using three significant figures (based on the provided $n = 2.42$):
$$v \approx 1.24 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$$

## 5. Final Result
The speed of light in diamond is:

$$\mathbf{v \approx 1.24 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}}$$
